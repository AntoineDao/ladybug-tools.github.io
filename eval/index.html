What is Ladybug Tools?

Ladybug Tools is a collection of open source plugins and web applications
that support integrated, performance-based solutions for the built environment. Of the available software for environmental design, Ladybug Tools is among the most comprehensive by connecting 3d modeling and flexible visual scripting interfaces to a variety of validated simulation engines.


Validated

Ladybug Tools is built on top of several validated simulation engines: Radiance, EnergyPlus/ OpenStudio, LBNL Therm/ Window and OpenFOAM.

Open and customizable

Ladybug Tools is open source, modular, and is designed to be customized into one's own tools and workflows.

Vibrant Community

Ladybug Tools is supported by a community of professionals, researchers and academics from all around the world. Read what our users have to say about Ladybug Tools.

90k+ Downloads

Ladybug Tools has been downloaded more than 90,000 times and is the 3rd most popular plugin for Grasshopper. It's also the winner of DynaAwards for the best Dynamo analysis package.

Inter-connected

Ladybug Tools connects to a comprehensive set of simulation engines and supports data transfer between them. So all geometry creation, analysis and visualization can happen right from the 3D modeling software.

Flexibile

Ladybug Tools is flexible across different stages of design and allows continuity of analysis with the increased project resolution.

Tools

We are the developers of Ladybug, Honeybee, Butterfly, Dragonfly, EPWMap and are contributors to other open-source projects including Design Explorer and the Glazing + Winter Comfort Tool.

Who is using Ladybug Tools?

Ladybug Tools is used by architects, engineers, consultants, simulation experts and academics for variety of projects. Here is a short list of some of the offices and schools.

Transsolar

By seamlessly linking existing and highly capable performance assessment software (for daylight, energy, airflow and thermal comfort) to design workflows, the insects are creating a design ecosystem in which architects and planners can shape their efforts to promote the highest levels of performance while satisfying all design objectives. Members of the design community, trained to be creative and now empowered to develop evidence-based strategies, will generate new concepts that will enrich the discourse about how best to serve both mankind and the natural environment.

- climate analysis (both macro and micro)
